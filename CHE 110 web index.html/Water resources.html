Bore Wells :>

Bore wells are commonly used in agricultural land and universities for irrigation purposes. Drilled wells are a source of clean water with very few impurities, making them ideal for greenhouse operations[1]. Bore wells are deep and narrow wells that are cased into the ground using a tube and are often equipped with an electric pump for irrigation[2].
PVC well screens are resistant to corrosion and are often used in shallow wells for agricultural purposes[3]. Low-yielding wells can also be used for irrigation in rural areas[4]. Proper design and construction of wells is important to ensure a reliable and safe water supply for agricultural and university use

Rain Water Harvesting :>

Rainwater harvesting is a process of collecting and storing rainwater from surfaces where rain has fallen upon. The collected rainwater can be used for various purposes, including irrigation, flushing toilets, washing clothes and utensils, and even for drinking after purification.
Rainwater harvesting can also be used on an industrial scale to provide the needed amounts of water for many operations to take place smoothly without having to deplete nearby water sources. It can also lessen the burden of soil erosion in a number of areas, allowing the land to thrive once again. The practice of rainwater harvesting has been around for thousands of years and has been growing at a rapid pace.Rainwater harvesting has many benefits and advantages. It is a sustainable water management practice that can be implemented on many different levels, from a simple rain barrel to a complex industrial system.
Rainwater harvesting can help reduce water bills, decrease overall water usage levels, and promote self-sufficiency. It can also help conserve water, reduce stormwater runoff, and solve drainage problems on properties. Rainwater is better for landscape plants and gardens because it is not chlorinated.
In addition, rainwater harvesting can help sustain groundwater levels, lessen the burden of soil erosion, and provide a cheap and reliable source of clean water in areas with water scarcity. Overall, rainwater harvesting is an important practice that can help address many water-related challenges.
